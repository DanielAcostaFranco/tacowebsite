Taco al Pastor

A Taco al Pastor is a popular taco known for its distinctive flavor and preparation method. The name "al pastor" translates to "in the style of the shepherd" and originates from Lebanese immigrants who brought the concept of cooking meat on a vertical rotisserie, known as "shawarma". However, the Mexican version has evolved with local flavors and ingredients, creating a unique dish that has become iconic in Mexican cuisine.

Unique Flavor!

Taco al Pastor is known for its unique flavor and blend of ingredients. The pork is marinated in a mixture of dried chilies, spices, pineapple, and vinegar, giving it a juicy, slightly sweet and spicy taste. The meat is cooked on a vertical spit, creating a crispy, caramelized exterior. When served, it is topped with fresh cilantro and onions, adding freshness, along with a squeeze of lime that enhances the flavor. The pineapple, which also cooks alongside the meat, adds a sweet contrast to the savory and spiced pork. All of this is served in a soft corn tortilla, creating a flavor explosion that ranges from spicy to sweet, with both crispy and juicy textures.
